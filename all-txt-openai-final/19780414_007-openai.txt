Untitled

Spin a silk cocoon and draw away

from impinging time—leave me

for a preset birth, the molt to come,

and imminent return to earth.

The moth that flutters in between

could (in some way) reflect

the moon's effect on littoral edge,

drawing in and letting go.

An unearthly breathing.

—Nancy L. McNair

April 14, 1978

David Litaker

The Davidsonian

Inside Art

Vol. IV, No. 6
