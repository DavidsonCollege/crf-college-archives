20 Friday, September 16, 1983 / The Davidsonian

FRIDAY, SEPTEMBER 16
PRE-MED STUDENTS: Scholarship information for Medical School will be available in the Conversation Pit, College Union. 11:20-2:00 p.m.

SENIORS: An informational meeting will be held in Chambers, Room 213, for anyone who is interested in applying for a Watson Fellowship. 2 p.m.

BIOLOGY SOCIETY MEETING: Dana Science Building, Room 204. Tonight our guest speaker is Dr. George Folkerts of Auburn University who will discuss “Life of the Southeast Pitcher Plant Bogs.” First meeting. Everyone is welcome! Refreshments afterwards. 2 p.m.

DAVIDSON CHRISTIAN FELLOWSHIP: Speaking at this week’s large group meeting will be Roger Edwards, Inter-Varsity staff worker for the Charlotte area. Join us for a time of worship, sharing, and learning. Morrison Room, the Union. 7 p.m.

BREAD FOR THE WORLD is sponsoring a weekend conference September 16-18 at Myers Park Baptist Church entitled “Outreach on Hunger.” The keynote address will be by Dr. C. Dean Freudenberger. Call Charlie the Chaplain for a ride. (Ext. 342). 8 p.m.

SOPHIE’S CHOICE: (Rated R). Pop film starring Meryl Streep who won an Oscar for her portrayal of a Polish death camp survivor. Adapted from the book by Davidson’s own William Styron. Admission is $1.50. Love Auditorium. 8 and 10:30 p.m.

DR. TOM AND SEUS: Don’t mime if I do. 900 Room. Sponsored by the Intimate Performance Committee. 10 and 11:30 p.m.

SATURDAY, SEPTEMBER 17
SALE ON THE GREEN: by the Davidson Garden Club. Bargain priced plants for your room, home-baked food, handcrafts, and other treasures. Don’t miss it! Located at Davidson Village Green, Concord and Main. Annual sale. 9 a.m.

4TH ANNUAL CHEERLEADER BBQ: Support the Davidson soccer and football teams on Saturday. Enjoy a delicious BBQ lunch on the practice football field. Tickets are $2.50 from any DC cheerleader or day of BBQ. Also sponsored by Coca-Cola of Charlotte. 11:00-1:00 p.m.

FOOTBALL: Davidson vs. Lafayette. 1:30 p.m.

DR. TOM AND SEUS: Speechless entertainment. 900 Room. 10 and 11:30 p.m.

Campus Events

SUNDAY, SEPTEMBER 18
DAVIDSON CHRISTIAN FELLOWSHIP: Join in a time of prayer and thanksgiving every Sunday. Lingle Chapel. 1:15 p.m.

DAVIDSON EMERGENCY RESCUE SERVICE: will be meeting at the Police Hut. Members and those interested in an EMT class should attend. 5:00 p.m.

MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 19
FIELD HOCKEY: Davidson vs. High Point. 4 p.m.

MUSIC!! The first Chamber Music Series will feature Stephen Pollock on the saxophone and John D. Coffey on the piano. A reception will follow in the foyer of Hodson Hall. Performance is in Hodson Hall. 8:15 p.m.

WINE AND CHEESE PARTY/FRENCH CLUB MEETING: First organizational meeting. Come find out what “le Cercle Français” is all about. Sample wine and cheese “à la française.” Morrison Room, Union. 8:30 p.m.

TUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 20
U.S. NAVY: In the Conversation Pit. 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

DCPC FALL PICNIC: For all Newcomers, on the Church Lawn. 6:30 p.m.

RESUME SEMINAR: In the 900 Room. 7:00 p.m.

WEDNESDAY, SEPTEMBER 21
U.S. NAVY: In the Conversation Pit. 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

DR. OHMER MILTON: Visiting scholar. Time and location to be announced.

OPEN LUNCHEON: In the 900 Room. 12:30 p.m.

FINE FILM: The Gold Rush. Charlie Chaplin (the actor, not the minister) stars in one of his most endearing roles. Liven up that weeknight in Love Auditorium. 8 p.m.

THURSDAY, SEPTEMBER 22
U.S. NAVY: In the Conversation Pit. 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

CROP MEAL: Expanded meal and host professors talk about different international concerns. In the 900 Room. 12:30 p.m.

RESUME SEMINAR: In the Morrison Room. 4 p.m.

DISCO: Rock and don’t stop it. 10 p.m.-12:30 a.m. Forget not to get your hand stamped in order to imbibe. 900 Room.

Leanne Stephens at this Friday’s performances here in Davidson.

The Roberts Bros. Circus will feature aerialist Leanne Stephens at this Friday’s performances here in Davidson. Trained ponies, dogs, and an elephant will also highlight the program. Student tickets are $3.00 in advance and $3.50 at the door. Tickets are available at the Union Desk. Working without a net, Leanne performs a routine of mid-air gymnastics with artistic showmanship that ranks her as one of the world’s foremost aerial stars.
