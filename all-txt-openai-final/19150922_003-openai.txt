ALUMNI NOTES. Wilson James McKay ('71) is a minister in Sumter, S.C. Philip H. Pitts ('71) has become judge of the District court of Selma, Ala. Thos. Crawford ('10) is seeing service in the European war as a Red Cross surgeon in the armies of the Kaiser. Chas. Whitely ('14) stopped over in Davidson a few days on his way home from Kentucky, where he has been preaching this summer. He expects to return to the seminary in a few days. R. B. Winn ('15) is working in the Y.M.C.A. at Sumter, S.C. Rev. F. D. Kerr ('09), of Newberry, S.C., has accepted the Professorship of Christian Ethics and Apologetics at Columbia Theological Seminary. Mr. John Browne Evans ('09), of St. Pauls, N.C., was married to Miss Alfreda Evans, of Bullock, N.C., on July 8, 1915. The Mooresville item in the Charlotte News of July 3 states that a social event of great interest throughout the state was the marriage Monday evening, June 28, at 7:30, of Dr. John Young Templeton, of Portsmouth, Va., and Miss Mary Williams, of Mooresville. Dr. Templeton is the youngest son of Mr. J. Y. Templeton. He is a graduate of Davidson College and also of Jefferson Medical College, and is a very successful young physician of Portsmouth, Va., and is held in the highest esteem by all who know him. Mr. I. M. Harden ('10) was married to Miss Mary Evans, of Abbeville, S.C., on September 1. Mr. Harden is a teacher in the Darlington school, of Rome, Ga. During a recent campaign for the organization of the alumni of A. & M., at Raleigh, eight live alumni associations were formed in various parts of the country, and plans are being completed for many more. The board of trustees have designated October 3 of each year as A. & M. College Day, and arrangements are now being made for a celebration at the college this year on that date.
