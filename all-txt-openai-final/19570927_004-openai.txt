SEVEN BRIDES TONIGHT

Sunday Speaks

NOTICE

Sunday Night “Seven Brides For Seven Brothers,” starring Jane Powell and Howard Keel, will appear tonight, Monday, and Tuesday, in the Ballroom of the College Union. Partially based on “The Rape of the Sabine Women,” an episode in Plutarch’s Lives, this musical has as its setting the Oregon territory of a century ago. Keel appears as a pioneer settler who comes to a frontier town to pick up a wife, along with some groceries. Jane Powell plays the part of his new mate.

Speaker for Vespers Sunday night will be the Reverend Samuel W. Newell, Jr., Th.D., pastor of the Davidson College Presbyterian Church.

Drawing for locations of fraternity houses will be Tuesday night at 7:30 in the Union Ballroom.

“Taste: sensations... excited... by the... action of the gustatory nerves...”

“And add this,” put in Mr. Wagnalls. “Taste: the faculty of... appreciating the beautiful...”

“That,” said Mr. Funk, “wraps it up. Mr. Wagnalls, will you join me in a Coca-Cola?”
