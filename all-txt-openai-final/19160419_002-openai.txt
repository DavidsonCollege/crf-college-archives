Y. M. C. A. MEETING.
At the Y. M. C. A. meeting Thursday night, N. P. Farrior, of the Senior class, gave a very interesting
and instructive talk on “The Call to the Foreign Field.” He spoke of those to whom the call comes, what
constitutes a call, and the kind of work to be done in the field. The speaker gave some startling facts
about the vast need of more workers, and the scarcity of laborers now in the field. It was announced that
at the next meeting some stereopticon slides of the Blue Ridge Student Conference would be shown and the work and
opportunities of the conference outlined.

GOLF TOURNAMENT BEGINS.
While baseball is in the ascendancy in Davidson's athletic world, golf is also becoming a popular and interesting sport. Carl
Anderson, a professional, visited Davidson some time ago and the interest in golf has been greatly increased by his exhibition.
The qualifying rounds for the annual spring handicap tournament were played Tuesday, April 6th. The winners were Professor Currie, Dr.
McConnell, and Dr. Arbuckle. Peters played Clark Monday, resulting in a victory for the latter. The finals will begin the
latter part of the week.

On Monday morning in the Eumenean Society the regular exercises were omitted and the first term officers of next year
were elected as follows: President, L. H. Eikel; Vice-President, J. R. Woods; Secretary, E. A. Solomons; Reviewer, T. J. Mitchell;
Treasurer, A. R. Craig. Two editors and an assistant manager for the Magazine were also chosen. They are: Senior Editor,
T. J. Mitchell; Junior Editor, L. A. Chambliss; Assistant Manager, H. B. Fraser. G. C. Bellingrath was elected a member
of the debating council.
