Freshman Class Put On Week By Court Of Control

Action Taken By Harrison Because Of Numerous Complaints

At last the “expected” has come! Davidson’s entire Freshman class, over 225 strong, will go on a strict campus beginning
tomorrow at noon, extending one full week until next Friday. This sentence, passed by Court of Control Judge Bennett, came
as a result of numerous violations during the past two weeks by a large majority of the first-year class, according
to Harrison. The violations were painting of the town water tank, general insubordination, failure to carry laundry, and attitude.

Restrictions

During this strict campus period, freshmen will be allowed to spend only thirty minutes after each meal in town. No
first-year man will be allowed to leave the city limits during this thirty-minute period, and no shows can be attended.
With the exception of the thirty-minute grace period, all freshmen will be required to stay on the campus. Judge Harrison
also stated that any member of the class caught violating this sentence would be dealt with severely by the Court
and that no exceptions would be made for any reason whatsoever.

This sentence marks the fourth straight year that such measures were taken in an effort to enforce and regulate freshmen
regulations. Year before last, the group was confined to a two-week campus as a result of numerous disturbances. Judge Harrison
also stated that complaints of violations and convictions of these disturbances have exceeded those of past years.

Fred Waring To Present Alma Mater

Fred Waring announced that his orchestra will play their original Alma Mater hymn for Davidson College on their Chesterfield program
on October 27 over the National Broadcasting System, at 7:00 P.M. EST. This information was wired to Mr. Lamar Burton,
Director of Public Relations here at Davidson. There will be a student gathering to hear the Alma Mater when it
is played, but as the paper went to press, no definite place had been decided upon for the meeting.

The song will be copyrighted by the Waring organization, but Davidson will have full rights to use it if they
wish; however, the college is under no obligation to accept the song if they do not like it.

Other Songs

Fred Waring has undertaken the task of writing Alma Maters for several other colleges, among them are Rider College, Trenton
College, and Ohio Wesleyan. Data concerning school traditions, sports, etc., has been sent to Mr. Waring in answer to his
request for such material. Mr. Burton explained in his correspondence to Mr. Waring that Davidson already had a fight song,
“O Davidson,” and that what was needed was an Alma Mater hymn. The choice, however, and type of the song
will be left entirely up to the decision of the Waring organization.

On the Mellow Side

Campus Body Will Gather Friday Night To Hear New School Song

There will be a student gathering to hear the Alma Mater when it is played, but as the paper went
to press, no definite place had been decided upon for the meeting.

The set of dances to be held November third and fourth will include a Friday night formal and a Saturday
night informal dance. As has been the custom for many years, the dances will be held in the armory at
Charlotte, N.C. This band has been in demand in the south and it has proved to be very popular in
college circles.

Dutch McMillin, popular young leader of the Duke Ambassadors, will wield the baton at the Homecoming dances. This band is
composed of fourteen students of Duke University. The clarinet is the featured instrument of the band.

Sam Wiley Will Lead Two Meetings Daily During Fall Services

Davidson Graduate Was Phi Beta Kappa and Omicron Delta Kappa

WILL MEET STUDENTS

Presbyterian Minister Now In Thomasville

Fall Services this year will be led by Rev. Sam S. Wiley, pastor of the Thomasville Presbyterian Church, and they
will continue from Tuesday, October 31 through Thursday, November 2. There will be two services each day, one at 10:05
in the morning and another in the evening at 7:15. In addition to this, the Reverend Mr. Wiley will be
available for interviews with anyone who may wish to discuss problems with him, or merely to talk with him as
a friend.

Service To Be Held

On Friday morning of this week, the chapel program will be led by Dr. Frontis Johnston. The faculty of this
chapel period will be devoted to the preparation for the coming Fall Services which begin on the following Tuesday. This
program is held in line with the request of Rev. Sam Wiley, who will be the speaker at the services,
that the student body should prepare for the Fall Services with prayer.

Dr. Price Gwynn, of the Davidson faculty, has recently had his address, “Making Teaching a Profession,” published in the North
Carolina Education Magazine. The address was originally made before the Institute of Public Relations when that body was convened at
Chapel Hill not long ago. The speaker made comments on the present status of teachers in North Carolina, stating that
the teachers have fulfilled these requirements admirably. He likewise made several suggestions as to how the present situation might be
improved.

While a student here, he was a member of the Y.M.C.A., Phi Beta Kappa, Omicron Delta Kappa, Sigma Alpha Epsilon,
and the Beaver Club. He was a Cadet Major in R.O.T.C. and was Captain of the Rifle team. After graduating,
Mr. Wiley was for two years Secretary of the Young People of the Synod of North Carolina. For several summers
he was a leader in the Boy's Club at Montreat, N.C. Last year Mr. Wiley returned to Davidson to lead
a “Prayer” for the Y Cabinet and the leaders of the hall prayer groups.

Quadwranglers Meet

The Quadwranglers’ Club, faculty organization, will hear an address from Dr. Hillhouse at their first meeting tonight at eight o'clock
in the Quadrangle Room. Dr. Hillhouse will discuss observations made on a recent voyage. His topic will be “Works of
several Scandinavian financial geniuses.”

Homecoming To Have Many Events

A southern conference football game, an alumni luncheon, a dance set, and a musical program will be the main events
of the annual Homecoming week-end, November the third and fourth. The luncheon that will be held in the banquet hall
of Chambers building will be the main feature of the program planned for the alumni and visitors of the college.
At this banquet, there will be four speakers. Presiding will be Mr. P. P. Mills, president of the Alumni Association.
The main speaker of the day will be Dr. W. P. Jacobs, president of Presbyterian College. His subject will be
“Something of his own choice.” He will be followed on the program by Dr. W. J. Plyler, president of Furman
University, who will speak for five minutes on athletics. After these talks, Mr. Duke Lalas will speak for five minutes
on alumni activities.

Dutch McMillin and his Duke Ambassadors will play for the dances which will be held on November 3 and 4.
Vincent Courtney will be the popular vocalist with the orchestra. Dutch McMillin and his orchestra are well known. The orchestra
played in different capitals of Europe last summer and was well-liked during its tour there.
