On Tuesday, January 30, the committee appointed to consider plans for the new gymnasium to be constructed at Davidson College met in Charlotte. Dr. Martin presented the plans for the proposed building as recently drawn up in New York; they were promptly accepted and he was authorized to call for bids for the immediate construction of the gym.

J. P. Williams, '16, was a visitor to the Hill for a few hours on last Saturday. Since his graduation, "Shor" has been in the offices of the Bureau of Municipal Research in New York City and later in Rochester, N.Y. He recently accepted the Secretaryship of the State Board of Charities for the State of Tennessee and will take up his duties there at once. He will make Nashville his headquarters for several months and possibly a year.

At a recent meeting of the Senior class, it was decided to adopt and wear a small lapel button bearing the class numeral, done in class colors, as a means of class distinction. The button is circular and will be gold and blue.

Due to the very cold weather of last Saturday and Monday, no class football games were played. The series will be resumed Saturday if the weather permits.

Miss Callie Christenbury, accompanied by Miss Marion Mosely, of Charlotte, spent the weekend at home.

WAKE FOREST GAME.
Davidson defeated Wake Forest, this being the first reverse the Baptists had suffered on their home floor since 1913. The game was brilliantly played and fiercely contested, and replete with spectacular plays. Both teams showed a strong defense, but Davidson uncorked an offense that was too much for their opponents. King and Childs gave trouble throughout the game and Reese and Walker held up the defense in fine style. "Dutch" Hengeveld was the particular star. His floor work stood out prominently and he was especially dangerous near the basket. The line-up follows:
