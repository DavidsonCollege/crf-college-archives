Elections are over for the campus’s leading weekly publication, the new staff has taken over, and now the readers of this column will have to look to the editor in chief’s column for the writings of Mac Hart. Mac left a mighty big pair of shoes for me to fill, both in writing this column and in running THE DAVIDSONIAN’s sports department. We will still have the same efficient staff to work with, so we hope to at least keep pace with Mac’s work.

Saturday will be a big day for Coach Derrick Barton and his tennis team. The racket wielders will lay a spotless record on the line against the Tar Heel racketeers from Chapel Hill. Should the Cats get by this one, they will walk off with the mythical state championship, a feat which no other Davidson team has accomplished in recent years to our knowledge. The netters have had little trouble thus far in the campaign in posting a string of ten straight victories. Davidson has gained a spot on the sports pages of the South and must be established as a threat for Southern Conference laurels. They have met and downed such noteworthy aggregations as Wofford, one of the nation’s top teams in 1947; Yale, Georgia Tech, and Presbyterian College. The University of Cincinnati was picked to give the Cats their strongest test of the season, but the Ohioans fell to Wildcat rackets to the tune of 8-1. Davidson can boast talent in each of its six positions. Number one, Bo Roddey, hails from Charlotte, prepped at Episcopal High in Va. Ted and Lenoir hold the number two and six positions, Whit Cobb, from Durham, is currently in the number three slot. Jerry Robinson, from Winston-Salem, and Mike Williams, of North Wilkesboro, handle the four and five positions very well. We may be going out on a limb, but we pick Davidson to defeat Carolina and cop the North Carolina State Tennis Championship.

Though most eyes will be focused on the tennis courts Saturday, that will not be the only athletic event on the campus. Also on tap for the day is a track meet with the Citadel. Coach Pete Whittle’s Bulldogs from Charleston will be highly favored to annex this Southern Conference melee. The locals should have little trouble in the distances with Jack Gray leading in the two-mile and freshman ace Blake Bradley showing clean heels to the one-mile aspirants. Bradley doubled up last Saturday against W. & L., and followed Gray for a second place in the home two-mile. In our mind, this laudable freshman has the possibilities of becoming one of the finest track men in Davidson history. He was one of the pace-setters on last fall’s cross-country team along with Bob Grube. He’s been beaten only once this spring in dual one-mile competition. Jim Vann has shown up right well this year in hurling the discus. Jim has also picked up more than several points in the shot put. Bill Brownson has yet to be headed in dual competition in his javelin heaving. With Brownson grabbing five points and Tim Hardin three with the sharp stick, Davidson usually garners a majority of the points in this field.

Speedy Newton turned in a 10.2 hundred-yard dash against W. & L., and a 22.7 two-twenty, which isn’t slow for any man’s track team. Newton apparently came from out of nowhere when he walked off with blue ribbon honors in the inter-frat track meet. He was here last year but was sidelined with a leg injury. But he has shown no signs of it this year as he has shown clear heels to many Wildcat adversaries. Coach Pete Whittle has built a worthy aggregation around this group of athletes that merit a strong turnout for this Southern Conference clash.

UNOFFICIAL STATE TENNIS CHAMPIONSHIP
Page Four THE DAVIDSONIAN Friday, April 23, 1948

Pictured above is Davidson’s No. 1 tennis player, Bo Roddey. Roddey will be pitted against North Carolina’s nationally ranked ace, Vic Seixas, here Saturday when the Wildcat netters tangle with Carolina for the unofficial state championship.

Introducing By DOUG HIX
Did you ever try to crack a “nigger toe” with your bare hands? Well, it’s about that hard to get Oliver “Bo” Roddey to tell of his heroism. Bo is a quiet, bashful, freckle-faced sort of guy who you would expect never had ever done much of anything except sleep. But when you see the smooth way he polishes off his opponents on the tennis court, it’s a horse of a different color. He seems never to get excited or in a hurry but he’s always there before the ball is. Bo started playing tennis when he was about nine years old and has been a champ ever since. He went to Central High in Charlotte one year and to Episcopal in Alexandria, Va., for two years, winning as he went. To mention a few of his trophies—he won the state championship at Central, went undefeated for two years at Episcopal, won the state junior championship two years, state men’s championship, Charlotte city championship, tournament at Roanoke, Southern Boys and Southern Juniors championship, and Mid-Dixie championship last year. Bo is a demon both in singles and doubles, but naturally, he does best in singles. You should have seen him give Coach Barton a run for his money the other day—of course, that was after he had already won two matches. At Episcopal, Roddey was a letterman on the basketball team for two years and on the football team for one year. He continued his basketball career at Davidson, earning a letter this year playing his same crafty, easy-going way on the basketball court as he displayed as a netman. He is a member of the K. S. social fraternity, Beaver Club, “D” Club, and works on the Scripts 'N Pranks. Bo was studying to be a doctor, but it seems there is a certain person in the science department that begged to differ with our assumption that Roddey is great—consequently, he is undecided as to his life’s work. When I asked him what he thought about our chances of beating Carolina, he was a little slow in answering. Bo has only been beaten three times since he has been here. How about coming down and seeing if our No. 1 netman can keep that record Saturday against Carolina.
