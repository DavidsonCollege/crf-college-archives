Cannon Pleased by Response As New YMCA Program Begins

The YMCA program at Davidson should be of interest to every student here, for we are all members of this organization. As pointed out in chapel last week, the Y offers numerous and varied outlets for student participation ranging from boys’ work, chapel programs, Sunday School extensions, to planning social events. Questionnaires returned indicate high interest, but there are still vacancies to be filled in the twelve programs being conducted. You men who have delayed in signing up for participation in the Y work, go by the YMCA office and do so as soon as possible. Hubert Cannon and the members of the Y Cabinet have spent a great deal of time and effort in planning a full program for the coming year. They will be somewhat handicapped this year by not having a full-time Y Secretary or Chaplain who has taken care of many of the details in the past. Let's get behind the YMCA this year! The plans of this organization, just as any other, cannot be successful if we, the members, do not give it our support.

No Dirty Business at Laundry; Gentle Care Given All Garments

Have you noticed your laundry lately? There seems to be a scarcity of missing buttons, mutilated shirts, and shrunk socks. What has been the cause of this very much improved service this year? To satisfy our curiosity we visited Mr. C. W. Stacks, the manager of the Davidson College Laundry, who told us of several changes that had recently been made to improve the standard of production. He pointed out that new shirt units had been installed last year and for the first time in many years collar supports and shirt boards were being put in every shirt to improve its appearance. Several new machines have just recently been installed in the dry cleaning department, where two-day dry cleaning service and one-day repair work are offered. There have been slight changes made in the delivery room where the partitions have been removed from the shelves and the laundry numbers clearly marked to facilitate picking up the laundry. It is interesting to note that this laundry has been in operation for over 31 years and now employs 44 persons in the various departments. Mrs. Carl Irvin has been with the laundry for 31 years and several others have been employed for over 15 years. We hope that this excellent service will be continued throughout the year, giving Don Mahy and the Scripts and Pranks no opportunity to find material from this source.

The Friendly Philosopher Speaks

(Editor's note—The following is an excerpt from a letter we have recently received from Nat Daniel, associate editor, who is now in the service.)

It isn't proper to admit it while you're a student, but while you're at Davidson you absorb something which can only be described by that somewhat overworked, almost trite phrase, "The Davidson Spirit." It rises above the individual, the social and the economic, and unites several hundred distinctly different people into a great bunch of men. It’s that extra something that makes your smile a little broader and your handshake a little firmer whenever a familiar face and voice carries you back to Davidson for a few moments. It's as old as the brick of Eu Hall, and as young as the somewhat bewildered, but friendly face of the youngest freshman. In some ways its meaning is different for each of us, yet some aspects are the same for all of us. It lingers long after we've forgotten the hard exams, the tough professors, the dull chapel talks, and all the other things we griped about. The result is that soon we flatter ourselves into becoming convinced that Davidson was at her best while we were there. It’s a funny feeling to be a "has been" when you weren't planning to quit so soon, but most of the aspects of growing up are crammed down our throats before we are ready for them. Davidson helps the growth process—including that of many of her critics, and even though most of us don’t realize what she has done for us until we leave, we should never underestimate her contributions to our lives. (None of the above should be interpreted to imply that I’m in favor of subsidized football—I’m not!)
