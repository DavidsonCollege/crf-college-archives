Fonda’s Music Mushrooms
By JOHN GLANDZ

There are a few performers who don’t fit into any prescribed genres; instead, they go and bridge cultures and create new pseudo-techno-auditory sounds that exude freshness and tormented sexuality. Jane Fonda is just that new gyrocompass of modern music — no genre but all genius. Through an inspired synthesis of pop tunes, literary works, and intense voice-overs, Jane Fonda’s Nuclear Workdown lives, inhaling and exhaling all of Ms. Fonda’s past achievements and firming and shaping the breasts of modern reality. Unlike past efforts, this time SuperJane has rediscovered socioexistence, and she speaks highly of it throughout.

Perhaps the most fascinating aspect of Workdown is its ability to transcend previous nuclear-oriented art, primarily through sheer lucidity. Jane’s use of Milton’s boss line, “Rocks, caves, lakes, fens, bogs, dens, and shades of death” poetically superimposed upon “Feel the burn?” in her “Helluva Workout” routine, stretches and startles the listener’s mind and muscles. Her “Atomic Horseman” begs for equestrian sensuousness when she instructs the listener/participant to get down on all fours and say neigh to a radioactive society. But even our Jane is no plain Jane. A gadfly like Fonda knows how to pull us out of our seats (literally!) and make us thrust and tremble, tremble and thrust to the beat of the revved-up and slightly altered theme song “On Glowing Pond.”

I don’t know — maybe I just didn’t understand what the raison d’être for Nuclear Workdown was, but I think this disc has a madreporitic reason for being. Jane’s real. Jane’s here. We’re there. If you’ve never given this reactionary political noctuid-turned-prophet a chance before, check her out. Workdown’s an intriguing example of music being produced today.

Singles and Albums

For the most part, there really are no top singles. No top albums, either. I already told you I liked “Da, Da, Da” and “Blinded by Science” and a third mystery single, but no monosyllabic, deranged, or nonexistent tunes have since been released. I checked the WDAV file for some more obscure albums, but damned if I haven’t reviewed them all. If I were you, I’d stick to Jane for the time being.
