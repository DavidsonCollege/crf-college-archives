4 [With the Alumni]

Rev. W. E. Hill, ’99, of Atlanta, has been called to the pastorate of the First Presbyterian Church of Fayetteville, N.C.

J. Boner White, '10, who will complete his medical course at Johns Hopkins in May, has received a two-year appointment in the Post-Graduate Hospital of New York City.

Among the teachers at the McCallie School, in Chattanooga, Tenn., is Woodfin Rampley, '13, of Elberton, Ga.

Rev. I. O. McCutchen, '95, of our Korean Mission, is at home on furlough and will spend the next four months at Princeton, N.J. He and Mrs. McCutchen are doing special Bible study work there.

Heman S. Shaw, who is now master in a large boys’ school at Haverford, Pa., was married on December 27th to Miss Grace D. Logan, of Leroy, N.Y.

Rev. A. L. McDuffie, '13, is principal of Beechwood Seminary, at Heidelberg, Ky.

NEW BOOKS IN LIBRARY

The Library has recently acquired several new books. Among them are several interesting books of fiction, including, “Penrod and Sam,” Booth Tarkington; “Somewhere in Red Gap,” Henry Leon Wilson; “Fortunes of Garin,” Mary Johnson; and “The World for Sale,” Gilbert Parker. Especially interesting is the “Anzac Book,” corresponding to the college annual, but composed by Australian soldiers in the trenches.
