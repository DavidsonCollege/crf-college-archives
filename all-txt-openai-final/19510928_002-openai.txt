President, Court of Control Evidence Mature Leadership

Student Body President McLean's orientation apology for his oratorical abilities had scarcely died on frosh ears before he demonstrated that his executive action needs no vindication. Despite several dousings from sophomores and juniors whose front was more one of cowardice than brass as they emptied water-filled waste baskets from the darkened windows of Watts’ second and third floors, Mack broke up last Friday night's “shirt-tail” run of the freshman class. Not, however, until after considerable damage to the dormitories and injuries to some of the Class of '55 had been inflicted was the upperclassman-incited run stopped. It would seem after such conduct that some upperclassmen need the orientation more than do the freshmen. It was encouraging, then, to see McLean step in and put a halt to this mobbish whim which had all the seeds of growing into a yearly practice.

In this same vein, THE DAVIDSONIAN endorses heartily Judge Johnny Guiton’s program for the Court of Control. It followed Mac's idea that the student body at large is not the group to discipline the freshman class. That is the purpose of the Court of Control, and as Guiton firmly asserted, that body can handle any situation which may arise. This mature attitude is one which the student body and Court have long needed and have been working toward. It seems to have arrived in this year's leaders.

Vesper Offering Y Revenue

Davidson's YMCA—the Young Men’s CHRISTIAN Association on a campus Christian in name, anyway—continues to function despite the student body's ignorance and lack of interest concerning its operations. Financially, the Y does receive a boost from the student body via its three sources of income—campus tax, an allotment from the college, and vesper collections. Even in the first two funds, the student body has no choice but to pay. It is in the vesper collection that the student body can give of its money if not of its time for the Y's functioning.

This year’s Y budget will probably call for an additional $1,200.00 in expenditure. Plans are to use this appropriation in payment of a part-time secretary for the chaplain and to maintain closer ties to the national Y organization by sending delegates to more Y conferences in this area. The student body alone is in the position to make this budget adequate through a realization of the opportunity to give through the vesper offering. That source stands as the only revenue which can be increased.

Truly, more things are wrought by our Y than this student body dreams of. Its work stretches from Unity Church and Brady’s Alley to the mission field and Hangchow Church in China. Its work is the work of The Master. Those who would serve Him will be conscious of these facts and aware of their ability to help as a part of the program of worship each Sunday night.

Success So Soon?

Like two Saturday tennis players who had proudly—if unexpectedly—found themselves with a volley going, the faculty and the student body hopefully—if not too confidently—await the beginning of the second week of a vastly improved chapel service, wondering which will be the first to muff the ball. Not that either is likely to under the present set-up, but time must erase apprehension.

The prompt follow-up by the subcommittee of the Faculty Committee on Religious Exercises to their report of last spring dispelled doubt that the findings would prove little more than findings, and that chapel would continue in much its same hodge-podge way. The faculty, in the report, showed a true concern over the programs—both as an instrument for helping to fulfill Davidson's function as a church college, and as a period in which students would look forward to rather than regret the entertaining and informative programs planned for them.

If the faculty was upholding its end of the bargain to the letter, the student body showed a cooperation which even the optimist did not figure earlier. The cat-calls, whoops, stomps, and related outbursts were gone in a twinkling. With promises of better planning and better programs by the faculty and administration, the student body took their step toward eliminating those irrelevant and irreverent practices which were thwarting the fulfillment of chapel's purpose. The results were the best services and most pleased audience in memories of those in the student body.
