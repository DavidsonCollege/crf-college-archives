12 Friday, April 8, 1983/The Davidsonian

FRIDAY, APRIL 8
BASEBALL: UNC-Chapel Hill. Home. 3 p.m. This game will probably be over with by the time you read this.

MEN’S TENNIS: Marshall. Home. 3 p.m. Ditto.

OUTDOOR CONCERT: Not just one band, but two (!): Right Profile (better than Bright Profile) and Let’s Active. Does that
make sense? Maybe they’re aliens. But hey, they serve free beer. Football practice field. 2 p.m.

COLLEGE BOWL PRACTICE: a.k.a. learning to answer questions before they’re even asked. Green Room. 4 p.m.

POP FILM: Time Bandits. A bunch of 21st-century Frito Banditos stumble across a homemade time machine mounted on a VW
chassis. Sounds deep. Love Auditorium. 8 and 10:30 p.m. $1.50.

MORE MUSIC: Cathexis (kuh-thek-sis), noun. 1. The investment of emotional significance in an activity. 2. A progressive jazz band. Take
your pick in the 900 Room. 10 and 11:30 p.m.

LIBRARY: Often overlooked on Friday nights but worth considering for those of you who predict intellectual sterility for the remainder
of this Frolicking weekend. Behind Chambers. Open ‘til midnight.

SATURDAY, APRIL 9
GRIEF, MEDCAT STYLE: Chambers Gallery. 8 a.m.-6 p.m. Does the local AI chapter know about this?

MEN’S TENNIS: VMI. Home. 9 a.m. Q.: Does anybody get up this early on Saturdays? A.: Yes. Tennis players and
aspiring doctors.

COED TRACK: Johnson C. Smith. Charlotte. And presumably runners.

WOMEN’S TENNIS: Baptist. Away. 1 p.m. Now that’s more like it.

CARNIVAL: Oh boy, there'll be popcorn, cotton candy, and a dunking machine. Can you say “dunking machine”? Chambers lawn. 12:30
p.m.

MUNCH OUT: Lots of ice cream, sundae style. 1:00. But beware: there will be a bunch of juggling yahoos who
call themselves the Locomotion Vaudeville. They will try to make you laugh after you have eaten yourself sick with ice
cream. Don’t let them. Chambers lawn still. 2 p.m. 25¢.

HOT AIR BALLOON: Rides even. Guess where. 3 p.m. 25¢

LET’S BOOGIE: Dance with Threshold. Drink some happy juice. Commons. 9 p.m. Is this where we finally get to frolic?

MORE MUNCHIES: Floats, sammiches (post happy juice pronunciation). Cathexis will again make joyful music. 900 Room. Until 3 a.m. Dance
ticket stub or $1.

POST 3 A.M.: Let’s keep it clean, okay?

SUNDAY, APRIL 10
CHURCH: Anywhere, anytime highly recommended. Atone for recent sins.

WOMEN’S TENNIS: College of Charleston (any relation to Charleston College?). Away. 1 p.m.

PRAYER MEETING: Eu Hall. 1:15 p.m. Also recommended.

CATHOLIC MASS: Lingle Chapel. 6:30 p.m. Couldn’t hurt.

STUDENT RECITAL: Starring Judy Redd, organist. DCPC. 8:15 p.m. Let the music lull you back into innocence.

EU MEETING: Eu Hall, strangely enough. 10 p.m. Everyone welcome.

MONDAY, APRIL 11
COLLEGE BOWL PRACTICE: Davidson’s finest. Green Room. 4 p.m.

BASEBALL: The Citadel. Away. 6:30 p.m. We’re there.

JUGGLING: Morrison Room. 7 p.m. Warning: The surgeon general has determined that juggling can be dangerous to your semi-circular canals.

AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL SPEAKER: Julius Chambers, Charlotte Attorney. Was detained by South African government. 900 Room. 7 p.m.

HUMES FILM: 2nd year. Probably Perkins. 4 and 7 p.m. Bet nobody shows up.

Y-STUDENT SERVICE CORPS: Morrison Room. 10 p.m. at night.

ONE WEEK: Since the Wolfpack won.

TUESDAY, APRIL 12
PREMEDICAL COMMITTEE: Conference Room. Noon. C’mon folks, why put on airs? Won’t “Premed” suffice?

CROP LUNCH: 900 Room. 12:30 p.m.

WOMEN’S TENNIS: High Point. Home. 2 p.m. E.S.T., 1 p.m. Central.

MEN’S TENNIS: UNCC. Away. 2:30 p.m.

COLLEGE BOWL PRACTICE: ... you know the rest.

FRESHMAN COUNCIL: Conference Room. 6 p.m. Topic: Should freshmen be allowed to stay up past 11:00?

CHAMBERS MUSIC: The noted Delos Quartet. Love. 8:15 p.m. Finally — some old wave for a change.

SGA: A.k.a. our illustrious leaders. Conference Room. 9 p.m.

DCF EXECUTIVE MEETING: Blue Room. 10 p.m.

WEDNESDAY, APRIL 13
COFFEE AND DONUTS: Where the Sunday pictures are. 9:40 a.m.

OPEN LUNCHEON: 900 Room. 12:30 p.m.

GOLF: Belmont Monastery Tournament. Away.

MEN’S TENNIS: Pfeiffer. Here. 2 p.m.

BASEBALL: Warren Wilson. Not here. 3 p.m.

COLLEGE BOWL PRACTICE: Don’t you guys ever stop? Enough of this repetition. College bowl practice every weekday in the Green
Room at 4:00.

WORSHIP SERVICE: Big Screen Room. 7 p.m. Highly recommended to those of you who sleep through Sunday for less than
virtuous reasons.

FINE FILMS: The Day the Earth Stood Still and A Trip to the Moon. Love. 8 p.m. Warning: The surgeon
general has determined that science fiction watching may be dangerous to your I.Q.

ICE CREAM: ATO. 10:30 p.m. Good stuff. P.S.: Don’t sit on the windowsills.

THURSDAY, APRIL 14
PERIMETER HOUSING: Requests due. Mr. Bill’s office. 8:30 a.m.

AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL: Two Russian students will talk about nuclear arms and human rights. Morrison Room. 4:00 p.m.

ALL STUDENTS: Select lottery numbers Thursday and Friday, or sleep in the gutter. Housing office.

DISCO: In case you don’t get all the frolicking out of your system Saturday night. 900 Room. 10 p.m.
