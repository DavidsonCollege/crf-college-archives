O. D. K. OFFICERS DEDICATE LIBRARY

Walter Wooten To Fraternities

Omicron Delta Kappa, honorary leadership fraternity, has elected its officers for the year. They are Ed Licks, president; George Marsh,
secretary; and Dr. Frontis Johnston, treasurer.

Members of the faculty approved heartily a faculty-student plan to move the library at an early date. The plan is
to pass the books through a line of students stationed along the newly constructed zone. Brief ceremonies will precede the
work and it is hoped that the donor of the new building will be present to participate. The president of
the library committee of the faculty will announce a suitable time for moving at a later date.

In an interview with Dr. Cecil Kenneth Brown, recently elected Dean of Students at Davidson, he said that he had
not had much time to become familiar with his recently acquired office. Dean Bailey realizes that he must fill out
his position and be able to look at a problem from the students' as well as the faculty's point of
view. Dean Bailey graduated from Davidson in 1922 with an A.B. degree. Later he studied at the Universities of Virginia
and Chicago. He spent a year at Johns Hopkins University, where he received his M.A. degree.

Dr. Durant, distinguished lecturer, author, and historian, will speak to the Davidson College student body on the subject “Philosophy and
the War.” The lecture will take place in the newly constructed hall. Dr. Durant first came into the public eye
when he published his book on the history of civilization. He is also a well-known lecturer. It has been said
that his lectures use history and philosophy to illuminate the basic issues of the day by showing how these essential
elements existed and how they were resolved.
