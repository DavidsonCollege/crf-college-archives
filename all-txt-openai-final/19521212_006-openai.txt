Davidson College Program Aired Weekly Over Charlotte Radio Station

By MOODY SMITH

Every Thursday afternoon at 12:45, Davidson College takes to the airwaves via the facilities of Radio Station WSOC-NBC in Charlotte.
“Davidson on the Air” is a part of station WSOC’s program policy, which allots 15 minutes of free radio time
per week to colleges in this area to use as they best see fit. Beginning this policy last year, the
station deemed it worthwhile enough to continue when colleges opened their doors again this Fall.

At that time, Professor Tyson, head of the Speech Department, became faculty advisor for the Davidson program. Under his direction,
a Student Radio Committee was formed, which includes Tad Currie, chairman, Vance Mizzell, and Howard Proctor. The purpose of this
committee has been, and will continue to be, to see that every phase of college life here at Davidson receives
a chance to have a hearing on the program.

So far this year, several dramatic productions and student discussions have been staged over the air. Last week, Danny Miller,
D.C.’s new basketball coach, was interviewed on the program. Professor Tyson stated in an interview with the Davidsonian that it
is his hope, and that of those students now connected with the program, that it will increase in scope in
the future in order to give better coverage of all sides of student life. He expressed the opinion that a
trend towards improved quality and coverage has been established and said that he was confident that it would continue.

Some of the programs produced so far have been “live,” that is, broadcast directly over the facilities of WSOC, but
most of them have of necessity been tape-recorded, due to the hour at which they are now scheduled. According to
Professor Tyson, it is his desire to see the program attain more recognition among members of the student body, who,
paradoxically enough, are probably the people least aware of it. In this way, Professor Tyson says he hopes to see
the day when the weekly radio production will constitute a major activity on the campus.

Mr. Raymond Tyson has been assisting and directing the group that puts Davidson's events, on campus and off, on the
air. Tad Currie is the student heading the project.—(Photo by Simons).

Tyson placed emphasis on the fact that the students in charge of “Davidson on the Air” are very eager to
allow any campus organization or activity to utilize the fifteen minutes or part of it if they have a program
that they would like to present. All that is necessary is to contact Professor Tyson or Tad Currie, and the
necessary arrangements will be carried out if at all possible. “Davidson on the Air is not a closed corporation by
any manner of means,” Tyson said, and added that he is very eager to see students who are interested in
working with or taking part in the program. “The more students taking part in the production or the planning end
of the program, the better and more comprehensive it will be,” he added.

Frosh Fagg, Flop and Fail As Sophs Sweep Series
By PAT GRAYSON

In a repeat performance of last year’s games, the sophomores defeated the freshmen in two events last Tuesday afternoon in
the Freshman-Sophomore Day competition. By winning the relay race and the football game, 12-7, the sophomores maintained their honor and
proved that the freshmen would have to continue wearing their hats until the end of the first semester. The tug
of war was officially called a tie because of the hoard of extraneous “pullers” from the bystanders.

The football game got under way at 3:45 p.m., with a selected team representing the classes. Passing was the only
offense throughout the game, although intercepted passes were the main ground gainers for each side. Dan Leach intercepted a freshman
pass to go for a touchdown for the sophomore team, and later Tom Hancock caught a fifty-yard pass to score
again for the sophomores. Bubba Martin snagged a pass in the dark closing minutes of the game to tally for
the freshmen. Another pass made the extra point good.

Frank Cenegy, Henry Brown, Bob Amos and Frank Mitchner copped the 400-yard relay race for the sophomores, speeding by Russell
Rogers, Bobby Wren, Van Julian, and Archie Joyner, freshman dash men.
