In the days of derbies, strong pipes, and high collars, Davidson had a baseball team which held her own against such teams as the University of North Carolina, the University of Georgia, Georgia Tech, and other strong colleges. The above picture is the team of 1910 and among those playing are some of the best athletes ever produced at Davidson. In those days baseball was the grand ole sport and a good baseball team meant much to a college. The above team was one of the best ever at Davidson and it causes us to look back on this team with much pride. Reading from left to right those in the picture are: Standing, J. W. MacConnell, faculty and college physician, now cashier of a bank in that city; DeWitt Kluttz, who as a letter-man in football, basketball, and baseball, was one of the best athletes at Davidson at that time; he is now engaged in the practice of medicine in Washington, N.C.; W. E. Mills, of South Carolina; F. B. Norman, of Charlotte, N.C.; Clarence Clark, of Clarkston, N.C., captain of the team, he is now in the banking business in Clarkston; Frank Sharp, the manager, of Greensboro, N.C. Kneeling are W. T. McClure, of Wheeling, W.Va., and D. M. Buie, of Red Springs, whose address is unknown. Reclining on the left is Everett Booe, of Davidson, N.C. Booe is now a professional baseball player in the big league. The mascot and player on the right are unknown.
