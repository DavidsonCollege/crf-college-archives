ALUMNI NOTES.

The following Davidson Alumni were up to see the game last Saturday: Mr. E. H. Harrison ('96), leading real estate man of Salisbury and former baseball player; F. L. Harkey ('15), of the Seminary at Mt. Pleasant, N. C.; “Ding” Bell, of Concord, one of Davidson's old ’Varsity pitchers; Russell Minter, A.M. ('14), member of the faculty of Linwood College, in Gaston County; “Doc” McKay, of Charlotte.

Rev. Carl Matthews ('95), pastor of the Presbyterian church at Pulaski, Va., was on the campus last Tuesday week. He has just been called as pastor of the First Presbyterian church of Mooresville, and we hope to have him as a neighbor before long.

TOWN ITEMS.

Mrs. F. G. Robinson left for her home in Wilmington Friday, after a visit to Mrs. A. Currie.

Miss Ella Young, Dean of Queens College, spent the week-end at Mrs. Arbuckle’s.

Mrs. Filton returned last week from Danville, Ky., where she has been spending the summer.

Mrs. Lem White, of Statesville, was visiting here last week.

Mrs. Arthur Finley, of Wilkesboro, mother of A. G. Finley ('18), was visiting here a few days last week.

Miss Fannie McBryde, sister of Mrs. Worth, fell in Brown-Knox’s store last Thursday and suffered serious and painful injuries. Dr. Stokes, of Statesville, and Dr. McConnell attended her.

Misses Myrtle, Flossie and Lilian Houston took the examination for Mecklenburg County teachers in Charlotte last Friday.

Mrs. C. M. Carson, of Charlotte, motored up for the game Saturday.

Miss Clark, of Clarkton, spent Sunday with Miss Sarah Wooten.
