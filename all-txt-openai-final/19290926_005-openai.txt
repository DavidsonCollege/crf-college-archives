Alumni Death

J. N. Van Devanter, ’15

News was received at Davidson this week of the death of James Nichols Van Devanter, at Albuquerque, N. Mex., death
occurring Sunday night, September 22, after an illness extending over several years. For some months he has been unable to
leave his bed, being afflicted with tuberculosis, which he contracted some years ago. The funeral will be at Fort Defiance,
Va., his old home, next Saturday afternoon.

The deceased was born in 1891, and entered Davidson in 1909, remaining here until 1915, when he obtained both his
bachelor of science and master of arts degrees. He taught at Staunton, Va., one year, and entered Union Theological Seminary,
where he remained only a year. From there he went to a resort in New York to combat the disease
which he had contracted, but failing to improve he left for Roswell, N. Mex., later going to Albuquerque. While at
the latter city he studied at the University of New Mexico. During his years out west he also did ranger
work in the Grand Canyon and mission work on the Indian Reservation near his location.

(NOTE: Numerous deaths among Davidson alumni occurred this summer, but these will be recounted in a later issue.—Editor.)
