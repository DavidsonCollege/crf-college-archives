Robert Nicholson

Icarus

Too young he learned to fly  
Cannot touch him, hold him.  
And soaring near the sun  
He singed his wings. Now, at night,  
He dreams, awakes, screaming  
With the pain that burns and burns.  
He will not be consoled.  
It fades and he returns once more  
To sleep.

Anthony Abbott
