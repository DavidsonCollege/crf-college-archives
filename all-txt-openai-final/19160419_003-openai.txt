WITH THE ALUMNI. PLANS FOR THE REUNIONS.

The plans for the reunion at the coming commencement of various classes are rapidly taking shape and the attendant details
being worked out. We publish herewith a list of the men who have agreed to take charge of the reunion
of their respective classes so that the members of each of these classes may correspond with them in regard to
the matter. From time to time this list will be added to as the men agree to help in the
arrangements.

CLASS OF 1876.

An interesting letter to the Alumni editor from Mr. R. M. Miller, Jr., of Charlotte, states that a reunion of
the class of 1876 is being planned to celebrate the fortieth anniversary of its graduation. Of the seventeen men who
graduated in this class twelve are now living, and eleven of these have promised to attend the reunion if possible.

ALUMNI OFFER MEDAL.

The Davidson alumni of Greenville, S. C., are offering a gold medal to the winner of first place in the
contest in oratory that was held in the Greenville High School on April 5th. The winner of this contest is
to represent the school in the Piedmont meet on April 21st, and the state meet on April 27th.

WHAT THE ALUMNI ARE DOING.

Dr. Robert S. Cunningham, ’11, who is now an instructor in Johns Hopkins University, was on the Hill for a
few days this week as the guest of Dr. J. W. McConnell. Dr. Cunningham is now at work publishing the
results of some of his research work in the University laboratories in the department of medicine. He was recently elected
a member of the Phi Beta Kappa Fraternity.

SHAKESPEAREAN PAGEANT.

On Easter Monday at 3:10 p.m., students of Queens College and Davidson College will give a pageant on the campus
of the first named institution, celebrating the tercentenary of Shakespeare’s death. The cast, which is composed of 150 people, will
dramatize four of Shakespeare’s plays under the direction of Dr. M. G. Fulton. The purpose of this pageant is to
stimulate interest in Shakespeare and to show the condition in the time of Queen Elizabeth. Characters represented by Davidson students
are as follows: “Wolsey,” L. A. Mullen; “Hamlet,” A. Dumas; “Shakespeare,” L. A. Chambliss; “Duke of Surrey,” W. C. Copeland;
“Prince Hal,” C. Llewellyn; “Touchstone,” R. C. Jones; “Falstaff,” L. G. Edgerton; “Shylock,” C. W. Angle; “Macbeth,” R. B. McKee;
“Romeo,” J. L. Payne; “Prospero,” H. K. Aiken; “Suffolk,” Latta Law; “Herald,” F. H. Baker; “Herald,” C. P. Childs; “Sir
Walter Raleigh,” A. S. Tompkins; “Othello,” W. G. Sayad; “Cromwell,” W. G. Morrison; “Antony,” G. D. Sparrow; “Lear,” R. H.
Bennett.
