Chambers Auditorium Slated For Renovation

Preliminary plans are being made for the renovation of Chambers Auditorium with construction to begin in May 1966. The remodeling will be made possible through a gift of $200,000 from the family of J. Spencer Love; the new auditorium will be named Love Auditorium. The college is now in the process of raising the additional $100,000 needed to complete the project.

Robert Currie, business manager of the college, pointed out the need for such improvements. It is impossible to have concerts in the present auditorium because of the size of the stage. Many performers will not appear here now because of the poor facilities. These improvements will make possible a more extensive artists series.

An extensive lighting program has been planned for the auditorium. There will be new house and theatrical lighting, which will include angled beam lights and more stage lights. Acoustical improvements have also been planned. The ceiling of the stage will be raised as part of this program, and there will be completely new sound and audio-visual equipment installed. An acoustical firm will be consulted this spring to make more recommendations.

The stage will be widened and lengthened. This will necessitate removing the columns which are now on the stage and moving the organ console to the side. The offstage area will be completely remodeled. A mezzanine will be constructed between the stage and the kitchen in the basement which will house dressing and waiting rooms. The remodeled auditorium will be completely air-conditioned.

Freshman Camp Scheduled

In order to provide more extensive and camping facilities, Freshman Camp will be held at Camp Thunderbird this fall. In previous years, the camp was held at Camp Stewart, and the number of incoming freshmen who could attend was limited.

Cane Campbell said the move would enable the “Y” to invite all freshmen to this pre-orientation exercise. The theme for this year's program will be “Who Am I?” with special emphasis placed upon the purpose of education.

“The only major change in this year's program will be an increased emphasis on the religious life at Davidson,” said Campbell. “In the past, religious life has taken second place to other aspects of campus activity at Davidson.”

The fact that Rush will be delayed until the end of the first semester will have some effect on the camp program. There will be less emphasis placed upon the fraternity situation, which Campbell thinks will make for a better program.

Davidsonian Assistant Sports Editor

Private sources have challenged the trustees of Davidson College to raise $50,000 in a matching fund for a $100,000 improvement of Richardson Stadium. Davidson College Director of Publicity Taylor Blackwell stated, “There has been announced to this office a challenge grant to improve the field pending. It has been pending for a couple of months.”

“There are certain details and public relations that have to be ironed out if this gift is from an outside benefactor that would have to be worked out with that benefactor.” President D. G. Martin was out of town and unavailable for comment on the gift.

Athletic officials have greeted word of the windfall with joy but some lack of certainty about just what is to be done. “I’m just very pleased that the money has been given,” said football coach Homer Smith. He indicated that the football staff was interested in improving the appearance of the field: “I’m sure the director of publicity would like to have some improvements in the press box. The football field itself is in fine shape. We have a good locker room. The only thing we as coaches are concerned about is the appearance of the field. I know that it will be a much prettier field.”

Athletic Director Tom Scott apologized for his lack of knowledge about the details of the gift, saying, “I don’t know. You’d have to ask Dr. Martin about that. He seems to be able to get the money he needs.”

“Dr. Martin mentioned something about getting $50,000 on a matching grant, but I’ve got to talk to him to find out what it’s to be used for.” Outgoing chairman of the Athletic Council and trustee representative to that committee, Dr. Tom Sparrow, commented on the gift, “I think some arrangements were made, but I don’t exactly know what. I don’t remember all of those details.”

“The council has recommended for a number of years the financing of some improvements in the lighting, dressing rooms, and adding some more showers.” Dr. Scott noted that the council has had several proposals before it for several years. “I guess Dr. Martin is just going back and getting some of our requests from four years ago.” He explained that the fall meeting of the Athletic Council four years ago had focused attention on adding lights to the stadium as well as making several other improvements.

“We played Furman one afternoon early in September when the temperature was 94 degrees. It’s a wonder to me that several of the players didn’t die, but fortunately just a few got sick from the heat.” He said that they recommended then the installation of adequate lights so that these early games could be played at night when the heat would not be so severe. He estimated the cost of lighting the field at $25,000.

Dr. Scott also pointed out, “We could have a pretty little field out there. We need to add some red and white. It’s just gray concrete now.” He said that improvements to the press box are needed because WBT radio is planning to carry the Davidson football games next year and because a number of the teams that Davidson plays have radio networks that carry their games.
