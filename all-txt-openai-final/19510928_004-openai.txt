Bert Anderton First Davidsonian Killed In Action
(Continued From Page One)

Judy, Bert’s older daughter, was in the room when her mother received the telegram telling of his death on September
2, the same day Judy came down with polio. Mrs. Anderton thought it was a telegram from Bert, informing her
when he would be home. Although Judy’s case is mild, Mrs. Anderton feared that if she received the full news
of her father’s death, she might suffer a relapse, so she told Judy that her daddy had only been hurt,
and that she would have the birthday party that she had looked forward to for such a long time. The
party was held last Saturday, with Judy wearing a pair of pajamas Bert had mailed from Korea before his death.

Bert was fighting his second war. Leaving the Air Force in December 1949, after two and one-half years of service,
the ex-tech sergeant became a football and baseball standout for Bill Storey and Charlie Jamerson. Bert signed up for ROTC
to receive a commission and served as battalion adjutant and vice president of Scabbard and Blade. He was a Distinguished
Military Student. Bert was also a member of the D Club and the SAE social fraternity. While at Davidson, Bert
and Corinne Anderton and their daughters, Elissa, now three, and Judy lived at 204 Cardboard Village.
