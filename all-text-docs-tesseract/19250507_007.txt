Peter Binney, by Archibald Marshall.

“A middle-aged Englishman who is  self-
made and a success from a material point of
view, envies the education and the free and
easy college life which he is able to offer his
After some difficulty he passes the en-
trance examinations, enters Cambridge Uni-
verstiy, and finds himself a fellow-student
of his own son,

son,

The possibilities of this un-
usual situation the author develops to the ut-
most, and as a result Peter Binney is one of
those rarities, a funny story that is genuinely
funny. Although this novel is familiar to
English readers, it has not appeared in this
country. that American readers
may enjoy this clever piece of work by
Archibald Marshall, Peter Binney is added to
the long list of his popular novels published
in this country.”
