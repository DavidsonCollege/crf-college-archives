DAVIDSON-QUEENS COTTAGE.

Work has been started on the con-
struction of the Davidson-Queens. cat-
tage at_ Blue Ridge, and it will he com-
pleted in time for the Y. M.C. A. Con-
ference, June 12 to 21. Oucens Col-
lege has paid in $250, of which $125 is
a gift from a Charlotte business man;
while Davidson has paid in $225. The
local committee is striving to actually
pay $400 of the necessary $500 this
year, and an effort will be made in the
next few days to collect the remaining
unpaid pledges.

CAMPFIRE ENTERTAINMENT.

On Saturday night an interested and
highly. appreciative audience witnessed
the entertainment given bv the local
Campfire Girls in Shearer Hall. All of
the pirls taking part were costumed in
their robes, decorated with symbolic
beads and emblems, and made by them-
selves. The setting of the stage was well
arranged for the scenes, green foliage
and candles being used.

Fire Maker and several Wood
Gatherers were initiated, after having
disnlaved something of their work.

Eight of the girls then executed sev-

«ral folk dances. among them being the
Swedish Folk, the 1-2-3, the French
Peasant, and a dance around the May
Pole, erected in the center of the stage
and decorated with red and black
streamers which were wound and un-
wound as they danced.

The music for the occasion was fur-
nished by the College Orchestra and
Mice Hattie Thompson.

The members of the local Camofire
who particinated were: Misses Willie
Camphell (Wichaka). Dorothy Finlay-
son (Amatola). Willie Henderson
(Natchanineo), Flizabeth Grev (Asshe-
weaua), Mary Thompson (Anouki),

Floice Martin (Artsila). Sarah Lingle
(Wenonah). Elizabeth Cranford (An-
ockous), and Miss Irena Foreman,
leader.
