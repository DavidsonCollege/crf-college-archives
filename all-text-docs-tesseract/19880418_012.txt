Dave Alyea’s puzzle corner

A traveller in India comes to an intersection of five roads. The signs posted next to four of the roads read:

This road goes to Calcutta

Both of these sentences are false

This road goes to Calcutta

At least one of these sentences is true

This road goes to Calcutta

Both of these sentences are true

This road goes to Calcutta

At least one of these sentences is false

Only one road actually goes to Calcutta, and the second sentences may be either true or false. Which road should the traveller choose?
