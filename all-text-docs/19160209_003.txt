WITH OTHER COLLEGES.

University of Mississippi—A  move-
ment for the tabooing of the cigarette in
Mississippi is now engaging the attention
of the citizens of that state and more
particularly the students of its State
University. Dr. W. S. Teathers, dean
of the medical department, and Dr. Al-
fred Hume, vice-chancellor of the Uni-
versity, have given their endorsement.
Every student and member of the fac-
ulty will be given an opportunity to sign
a petition, urging the Legislature to en-
act such a law. A number of students
who use cigarettes have expressed them-
selves as eager to sign such a petition.

North Carolina A. & M.—The de-
bating team of the North Carolina A.
& M. was victorious in a debate with
Auburn, held in Raleigh, Friday night.
January 28. The North Carolina A. &
M. will debate with Auburn next year
at Auburn.

University of Louisiana—The Ath-
letic Council of the University of Louis-
iana has drawn up plans for a new ath-
letic field which will provide an ade-

ball. The grouping of the various ath-
letic activities of the University into one
field will be a great step forward.
