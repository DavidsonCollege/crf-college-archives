President, Court of Control

Evidence Mature Leadership

Student Body President McLean's orientation apology for
his oratorical abilities had scarcely died on frosh ears before he
demonstrated that his executive action needs no vindication.

Despite several dousings from sophomores and juniors
whose front was more one of cowardice than brass as they
emptied water-filled waste baskets from the darkened windows
of Watts’ second and third floors.. Mack broke up last Friday
night's “‘shirt-tail'’ run of the freshman class. Not, however,
until after considerable damage to the dormitoriés and injuries
to some of the Class of 55 had been inflicted was the upper-
classman-incited run stopped.

It would seem after such conduct that some upperclassmen
need the orientation more than do the freshmen. It was en-
couraging, then, to see McLean step in and put a halt to this
mobbish whim which had all the seeds of growing into a yearly
practice.

In this same vein. THE DAVIDSONIAN endorses hearti-
ly Judge Johnny Guiton’s program for the Court of Control.
It followed Mac's idea that the student body at large is not the
group to discipline the freshman class. That is ‘the purpose of
the Court of Control, and as Guiton firmly asserted. that body
can handle any situation which may arise. This mature attitude
is one which the student body and Court have long needed and
have been working toward. It seems to have arrived in this year's
leaders.

Vesper Offering Y Revenue

Davidson's YMCA—the Young Men’s CHRISTIAN As-
sociation on a campus Christian in name, anyway—continues to
function despite the student body's ignorance and lack of interest
concerning its operations.

Financially. the Y does receive a boost from the student
body via its three sources of income—campus tax. an allotment
from the college, and vesper collections. Even in the first two
funds, the student body has no choice but to pay.

It is in the vesper collection that the student body can give
of its money if not of its time for the Y's functioning. This
year’s Y budget will probably call for an additional $1,200.00
in expenditure. Plans are to use this appropriation in payment
of a part-time secretary for the chaplain and to maintain closer
ties to the national Y organization by sending delegates to more
Y conferences in this area. The student body alone is in the
position to make this budget adequate through a realization of
the opportunity to give through the vesper offering. That source
stands as the only revenue which can be iricreased.

Truly, more things are wrought by our Y than this stu-
dent body dreams of. Its work stretches from Unity Church and
Brady’s Alley to the mission field and Hangchow Church in
China. It’s work is the work of The Master. Those who would
serve Him will be conscious of these facts and aware of their
ability to help as a part of the program of worship each Sunday
night.

Success So Soon?

Like two Saturday tennis players who had proudly—if
unexpectedly—found themselves with a volley going, the faculty
and the student body hopefully—if not too confidently—
await the beginning of the second week of a vastly improved
chapel service, wondering which will be the first to /muff the
ball.

Not that either is likely to under the present set-up, but
time must erase apprehension. The prompt follow up by the
subcommittee of the Faculty Committee on Religious Exercises
to their report of last spring dispelled doubt that the findings
would prove little more than findings, and that chapel would
continue in much its same hodge-podge way. The faculty, in
the report, showed a true concern over the programs—both as
an instrument for helping to fulfil Davidson's function as a
church college, and as a period in which students would look
forward to rather than regret the entertaining and informative
programs planned for them.

If the faculty was upholding its end of the bargain to the
letter, the student body showed a cooperation which even the
optimist did not figure earlier. The cat-calls, whoops, stomps,
and related outbursts were gone in a twinkling,. With promises
of better planning and better programs by the faculty and ad-
ministration, the student body took their step toward eliminat-
ing those irrelevant and irreverant practices which were thwarted
ing the fulfillment of chapel's purpose. The results were the best
services and most pleased audience in memories of those in the

student body.
