DAVIDSON MEN ELIGIBLE
RHODES SCHOLARSHIPS

A Rhodes Scholar will be appointed this
autumn in cach of 32 states, including the
two Carolinas, Virginia, Tennessee, Hlorida,
Kentucky. West Virginia, Maryland, and
Louisiana. Said Scholars will take up resi-
dence at the University of Oxford, 1923.
Davidson alumni and undergraduates who
were born between Oct. 1, 1898 and Oct. 1,
1904 are eligible. The three qualities
stressed in the apointment are, (1) Qualities
of manhood, character, and leadership; (2)
Literary and scholastic attainments; (3)
Physical vigor, as shown by interest in out-
door sports, etc. The annual stipend of
each Scholar at present is 350 pounds ster-
ling.

Especially are alumni urged to apply, who
had a ‘good, all-round college record, who
included an ancient language in their col-
lege course, and who have had further
valuable experience since graduation.

All whe wish to apply should send their
names at once to Prof. Thos. W. Lingle
who, will furnish any information desired in
this connection, and who will submit all
names to the Davidson Faculty Oct. 9th for
the endorsement of a maximum of three ap-
plicants for appointment in any one state.

At present Davidson has one Rhodes
Scholar at Oxford, Mr. Shelby McCloy,
‘19, appointed from the state of Arkansas.
