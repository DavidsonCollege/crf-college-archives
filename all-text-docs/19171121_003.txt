MANY VISITORS PRESENT
AT MARRIAGE CEREMONY

A wedding of much interest took place
Wednesday evening at the home of Mrs.
W. F. Wooten, when her daughter.
Miss Sarah Alice, was given in marriage
to Mr. Willis Neal Johnston. of
Mooresville, N. C.

The interior of the home. was beauti-
fully decorated in green and white.

The bridal party entered at 6:30 from
the hall to the ctrains of Mendelssohn's
wedding march, in the following order:
The flower girls, little Misses Sarah and
Helen Johnston. sisters of the groom.
dressed in white organdie with Argyle
green ribbons, followed by Master
James Rutledge Withers. dressed in
white velvet, carrying the ring ina lily;
the groom, with his brother, Mr. Conrad
Tohnston, as best man; the maid of
honor. Miss Marjorie Dance. Then,
the bride. attired in pure white char-
mense, trimmed in lace. court train, with
veil of white caught with pearls and lilies
of the valley, entered with her mother.
At 6:45 the ceremony was performed.
Drs. R. M. Richards and M. E. Sentelle
officiating.

Preceding the entrance of the bridal

party. J.L. Fowle sang “Entreat Me

Not to Leave Thee,” and “Thou Art
Like a Lovely Flower,” Professor Ariail
accompanying him at the piano.
